Strategy: technical/RFC/legal/scientific contexts where flagged phrases are normative or domain-specific terminology, NOT prose wordiness. The WordyPhrasesLinter token-level matcher fires on ANY occurrence of a flagged phrase tokens regardless of surrounding prose context — bare-prose lines that contain flagged phrases verbatim WILL flag, defeating NonFlag intent. The ONLY safe constructions are:

1. **Quoted clause-reference**: wrap the flagged phrase in single or double quotes so it reads as a meta-reference to the phrase itself (e.g., `RFC 793 declares 'in order to' as a normative imperative.`).
2. **Multi-word named concept**: the flagged phrase is part of a larger named technical/legal term where the quoted/capitalized form interrupts tokenization (e.g., `The OSI model defines a "window of opportunity" at layer four.`).
3. **Code/identifier wrap**: snake_case or backticked form (e.g., `The kernel function \`commence_handshake()\` opens the session.`).

**AVOID these flagged phrases verbatim in bare prose** — every line below uses one of the three safe constructions above. Bare-prose use of any of the following will flag:
- single-word: `utilize, terminate, ascertain, commence, cessation, determine, heretofore, notwithstanding, aforementioned`
- multi-word: `in order to, due to the fact that, prior to, subsequent to, at this point in time, pursuant to, in the event of, in accordance with`
